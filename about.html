About

What Northwatch is, why it exists, and the standard applied to every number on this site.

What this is

Northwatch is an independent civic data resource tracking structural economic, demographic, and social metrics for Canada. It exists to answer one question with data rather than opinion: what is actually happening to the country, and why.

Why it exists

Public institutions in Canada produce an enormous volume of data — on debt, demographics, productivity, housing, social trust. That data rarely reaches people in a form that is honest, accessible, and free of partisan framing. Northwatch exists to close that gap: one destination, sourced entirely from public institutions, updated as new data is released.

Who runs it

Northwatch is independently maintained. It is not affiliated with any political party, government body, or corporate interest, and accepts no advertising. The project is structural in orientation, not partisan — the mechanisms tracked here (fiscal, demographic, productivity) predate the current political cycle and are not attributed to any one party.

Sourcing standard

Every metric is drawn from public institutional sources: Statistics Canada, the Bank of Canada, the Parliamentary Budget Officer, the OECD, IMF, and equivalent bodies. Each chart lists its source and a last-verified date. Nothing is modeled or invented. Full detail on how metrics are selected and scored is in the Methodology page; corrections are logged in the Changelog.
